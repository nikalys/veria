# Banner / Announcement Bar

**Definition**: A distinct, full-width, permanently visible strip of content placed at the absolute top of the viewport (often sitting above the main Navigation Bar). It is used to broadcast critical global information, marketing promotions, or legal warnings to every user across the entire digital product.

**History**: Banners are the digital equivalent of breaking news tickers on television. In the early 2010s, they became a massive marketing trend used to drive Black Friday sales ("Use code FREESHIP for 20% off"). Around 2018, they transformed from a marketing tool into a strict legal necessity due to the European Union's GDPR ruling, forcing every website on earth to feature a persistent banner at the top or bottom of the screen begging for "Cookie Consent".

**Variations**:
- **Marketing / Promotional Banner**: Placed above the Navbar. Highly vibrant (often a brand accent color like neon yellow or bright purple) containing a short text hook ("Our summer sale is live!") and a link. Sometimes removable via an 'X' icon.
- **System Outage Alert**: A stark red or orange banner used exclusively by SaaS products to announce massive global issues (e.g., "We are currently experiencing degraded performance across our API servers.").
- **Cookie Consent Banner**: The legal necessity. Often placed at the absolute bottom of the screen, floating above everything else until the user explicitly clicks "Accept All" or "Manage Preferences".
- **Sticky vs. Dismissible**: A sticky banner remains on screen as the user scrolls. A dismissible banner allows the user to close it, saving a browser cookie so it never returns on subsequent visits.

**Use Cases**:
- **Sales & Promotions**: The most effective way to announce a sitewide discount without altering the content of 50 individual pages.
- **App Updates**: "We've updated our terms of service, click here to read."
- **Global Error Handling**: Unlike a Toast (which handles a specific user action), a Banner handles platform-wide failures that affect everyone.
