# Card

**Definition**: A highly flexible, bounded UI container (usually rectangular and resembling a physical playing card or business card) that groups related pieces of information about a single subject. It acts as an entry point to more detailed information, typically containing a combination of an image, a title, a short description, and a Call to Action (like a button or simply making the entire card clickable).

**History**: While modular content containers existed earlier, the "Card" as a distinct, universally recognized UI paradigm was formalized by social networks (Pinterest, Twitter, Facebook) in the early 2010s to handle massive activity feeds of disparate content (a photo, a link preview, a text status). Google's Material Design (2014) physically codified them, stating that cards should have a slight drop shadow (`elevation`) to signify that they are a distinct layer floating above the background canvas.

**Variations**:
- **Image-Heavy Card (Media/News)**: A massive full-bleed image on top, with a title and brief excerpt below. The standard for blog posts and news feeds.
- **Product Card (E-Commerce)**: Usually features a square product image on a white background, the brand, the product name, the price, and an "Add to Cart" button. Often includes a small badge (e.g., "Sale" or "New") floating in a corner.
- **Profile / Avatar Card**: Contains a circular avatar image, a user's name, their job title, and social links. Used for team pages or social directories.
- **Horizontal Card**: Instead of stacking image > text vertically, the image is on the left and the text is on the right. Excellent for dense lists where vertical space is at a premium.

**Use Cases**:
- **Social Media Feeds**: The fundamental building block of infinite scroll. Every post, tweet, or pin is technically a card.
- **Dashboards & Analytics**: Often used to contain distinct widgets or metric readouts in a Bento Grid layout.
- **Portfolios**: Presenting case studies as clickable, visual entry points.
