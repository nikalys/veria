# Timeline

**Definition**: A highly structured visual layout used to display a sequence of events in chronological (or reverse-chronological) order. It relies on a strong central or side axis (the "line") connecting distinct nodes or cards (the "events"), allowing users to easily understand the flow of history, a future roadmap, or the individual steps taken in a complex process.

**History**: A classic information visualization technique native to history textbooks and project management charts long before the web. In digital interfaces, they were famously popularized and permanently ingrained in UX vocabulary by Facebook's massive 2011 "Timeline" redesign (which turned a simple wall of text into a literal two-column chronological biography centered on a central spine). Today, they are ubiquitous for process tracking rather than personal histories.

**Variations**:
- **Vertical Centered Timeline**: The classic Facebook style. A continuous vertical line runs down the absolute center of the page. Content cards alternate left and right off the line, connected by small dots.
- **Vertical Left-Aligned Timeline**: Often used when content is text-heavy or screen width is constrained (mobile). The continuous line runs down the far left margin, and the event data expands to the right. Highly functional for logs and history.
- **Horizontal Process Timeline**: A line running left-to-right across the top or center of a viewport. Typically used for short sequences (4-6 items) like a company history ("1998 -> 2005 -> 2015 -> Today").
- **Activity Stream / Audit Log**: A micro-timeline used in B2B SaaS. It tracks exactly who did what to a specific file or ticket (e.g., "User A created at 10:00am" -> "User B edited at 10:15am").

**Use Cases**:
- **Corporate History / About Pages**: The standard way organizations tell their story, plotting major milestones (funding rounds, product launches, acquisitions) along a central spine.
- **Product Roadmaps**: Essential for open-source projects or SaaS companies wanting to publicly display what features they shipped in Q1, and what they plan to ship in Q3.
- **E-Commerce Order Tracking**: The highly stressful utility of the timeline: "Order Placed -> Shipped -> Out for Delivery -> Delivered", providing comfort to anxious buyers.
- **Project Management / CRM Log**: Tracking previous interactions with a lead, or the changing status of a Jira ticket over a two-week span.
