# Logos / Social Proof Strip

**Definition**: A horizontal layout pattern composed entirely of brand logos representing a company's past clients, active partners, investors, or major news publications that have featured them ("As Seen On..."). It is often the simplest and most universally deployed form of authority building in digital design.

**History**: Much like testimonials, this pattern migrated from business-to-business (B2B) print marketing into web design. Startups recognized that "borrowing authority" was a growth hack: placing a massive Google or Fortune 500 logo on their otherwise unknown startup site immediately conferred a sense of legitimacy to skeptical visitors. It became a ubiquitous, unquestioned requirement for every SaaS landing page built after 2010.

**Variations**:
- **The "Hero" Logo Strip**: By far the most common implementation. Placed immediately beneath the primary Hero section (or structurally part of it). A gray, desaturated row of 4 to 6 massive, recognizable logos.
- **The "As Seen In" publication strip**: Instead of client logos, it features logos from *TechCrunch*, *Forbes*, *The New York Times*, or *Wired*, proving that the company is newsworthy.
- **Infinite Scrolling Marquee / Ticker**: If a company has 20 recognizable clients, they cannot simply stack them in a massive grid without bloating the page. Instead, the logos are placed inside a continuous, slowly auto-panning horizontal marquee loop.
- **The Investor Block**: Specifically used by early-stage startups aiming to hire talent or raise subsequent capital: "Backed by Andreessen Horowitz, Sequoia Capital, Y Combinator."

**Use Cases**:
- **B2B SaaS Landing Pages**: "Trusted by over 10,000 forward-thinking teams including..."
- **Consulting / Agency Portfolios**: Proving to a prospective client that you are capable of handling enterprise-level contracts by showing the massive corporations you currently work for.
- **Open Source Projects**: "Used in production by major tech companies."
- **Consumer Hardware / Kickstarter**: "As reviewed by MKBHD, The Verge, Engadget."
