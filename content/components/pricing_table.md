# Pricing Table

**Definition**: A highly specialized, side-by-side layout pattern designed exclusively to compare different tiers of a product or service. Unlike a standard data grid, a Pricing Table is fundamentally a marketing component. Its goal is to guide the user toward the most profitable tier (the "Golden Middle") using psychological anchors, visual hierarchy, and distinct Call to Action (CTA) buttons.

**History**: Originally derived from print brochures comparing different magazine subscriptions. On the web, it became the undisputed engine of the SaaS (Software as a Service) revolution. Because selling intangible digital subscriptions is incredibly difficult, designers turned the checkout process into a standardized menu. The "Three-Tier Pricing Table" (Basic, Pro, Enterprise) became one of the most exhaustively A/B tested UI patterns in existence.

**Variations**:
- **The Three-Tier Classic**: Three distinct cards placed side-by-side. The left tier is cheap (the anchor), the middle tier is slightly larger and "Highlighted" (often labeled "Most Popular"), and the right tier is prohibitively expensive (Enterprise).
- **Toggle Pricing**: The table includes a critical "Monthly vs. Annual" toggle switch at the top. Flipping it dynamically recalculates all the prices in the cards below, usually heavily discounting the annual option.
- **Credit / Usage Slider**: Instead of static monthly tiers, the pricing table features a massive horizontal Slider ("How many emails will you send?"). Dragging the slider dynamically calculates the price directly inside the card.
- **Pay-As-You-Go Calculator**: Strips away the three cards entirely in favor of a utilitarian dashboard predicting costs based on specific developer metrics (Compute, Storage, Bandwidth) heavily used by AWS or Vercel.

**Use Cases**:
- **SaaS Subscriptions**: The literal financial engine of modern software. Users will skip the entire landing page specifically to find this table to see if the product is in their budget.
- **Service Packages**: Used by agencies or freelancers (e.g., Bronze, Silver, Gold Web Design Packages).
- **Event Ticketing**: Comparing "General Admission" vs. "VIP" access features.
