# Vaporwave

**Definition**: A highly specific, surreal, and ironic internet-born aesthetic characterized by retro 1980s/1990s tech imagery, pastel color palettes (predominantly pinks, purples, and cyan), classical Greco-Roman statues, early 3D computer graphics, and a distinct feeling of consumerist nostalgia and alienation. 

**History**: Emerged around 2010 on Tumblr and Reddit, initially as a microgenre of electronic music that slowed down and chopped up 80s elevator music, smooth jazz, and corporate lounge tracks. The visual aesthetic evolved alongside it as a critique of techno-optimism and late capitalism. While distinct from Y2K (which is optimistic and bubbly) and Synthwave (which is dark and neon), Vaporwave is uniquely defined by its surreal, dreamlike atmosphere and juxtaposition of ancient art with obsolete technology (like Windows 95 interfaces and VHS glitches).

**Variations**:
- **Classic Vaporwave (Aesthetic)**: The textbook definition: Greco-Roman busts (often with Glitch effects), pastel grids, palm trees, Fiji water bottles, and Japanese text (Kanji).
- **Mallsoft**: A softer, more ambient subgenre focusing heavily on empty 1980s consumer spaces (dead malls, neon escalators, liminal spaces) washed in pastel light.
- **Seapunk**: A closely related aquatic precursor featuring early 3D dolphins, oceanic imagery, yin-yang symbols, and vibrant turquoise/blue palettes.
- **Glitch / VHS Degradation**: Extremely heavy use of chromatic aberration, scanlines, tracking distortion, and tracking errors overlaid onto pastel backgrounds.

**Use Cases**:
- **Music Industry / Record Labels**: Essential visual language for lo-fi, synth, and electronic artists selling vinyls or cassette tapes.
- **Streetwear / Indie Fashion Brands**: Reclaiming the ironic 90s aesthetic (e.g., Arizona Iced Tea branding).
- **Crypto / Web3 and NFT Art**: Heavily adopted (often unironically) by early NFT communities blending classical statues with digital scarcity.
