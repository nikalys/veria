# Claymorphism

**Definition**: A soft, friendly 3D aesthetic where UI elements (buttons, cards, avatars, and floating assets) appear to be made of brightly colored, matte-finish clay or soft plastic. It utilizes a specific combination of inner and outer shadows to create puffy, rounded, extrusion effects without hard edges or glossy highlights.

**History**: Emerged around 2021 as a playful synthesis of Neumorphism and the explosion of 3D illustration in web design. Where Neumorphism looked like precise, machined plastic pressing out of a dashboard, Claymorphism looked hand-molded and buoyant. It was heavily popularized by 3D asset libraries (like humans.com) and adopted rapidly by startups tired of 2D flat design but unwilling to use harsh Neobrutalism.

**Variations**:
- **Pure Clay**: The classic CSS implementation: a solid pastel or vibrant background color, a dark inner shadow on the top-left, a light inner shadow on the bottom-right, and a large, soft outer drop shadow to lift it off the canvas.
- **3D Asset Clay**: Using pre-rendered or WebGL interactive 3D elements (characters, icons, coins, shields) alongside 2D UI. The 2D UI itself may or may not be claymorphic, but the primary visual language is driven by the 3D clay assets.
- **Glass-Clay**: The puffy, rounded extrusion of clay, but rendered with semi-transparent frosted glass (often seen in high-end iOS app concepts).

**Use Cases**:
- **B2C FinTech / Crypto**: Making complex, intimidating financial subjects (like trading, savings, Web3) feel like a friendly, accessible game.
- **Educational Tech (EdTech)**: The toy-like aesthetic is perfect for learning platforms, appealing to both children and adults by lowering entirely the intimidation factor.
- **Gamified Onboarding**: Used in app walkthroughs where friendly 3D clay hands point to features or hold clay phones.
- **Playful SaaS Landing Pages**: Replacing abstract 2D Corporate Memphis vectors with friendly, bouncy 3D clay elements to show product benefits.
