# Corporate Memphis (Alegria)

**Definition**: A highly distinct, polarizing vector illustration style used predominantly by tech companies and SaaS platforms. Characterized by flat, geometric human figures with wildly disproportionate bodies (tiny heads, massive swooping limbs) and non-natural skin tones (blue, purple, orange) performing abstract, joyful tasks in a void. Designed to look globally inclusive, friendly, and entirely unthreatening.

**History**: Originated around 2017 when Facebook commissioned the design agency Buck to create the "Alegria" (joy) system to unify their marketing and product illustrations. It spread like wildfire across the entire tech industry because it was highly scalable, easy to reproduce, and mathematically solved the "diversity" problem in illustration by removing actual human skin tones entirely. Eventually dubbed "Corporate Memphis" (referencing the vibrant 80s Memphis Group) by critics who felt the style had become a soulless, ubiquitous tech trope. 

**Variations**:
- **Alegria (The Original)**: Huge, sweeping limbs, tiny heads, abstract floating background elements (plants, geometric shapes), and pure flat colors with zero outlines.
- **Line & Fill Memphis**: A variation that adds thin black strokes to the shapes and slightly offsets the color fills, giving it a slightly more hand-drawn, "indie" feel while retaining the impossible proportions.
- **3D Memphis / Puffy Tech**: Taking the flat Corporate Memphis proportions but rendering them in soft, matte 3D (often clay-like). Still features purple people high-fiving over massive abstract charts.

**Use Cases**:
- **B2B SaaS Landing Pages**: "The" aesthetic for selling enterprise software in the late 2010s. Friendly, non-intimidating, and scalable.
- **Empty States & Onboarding**: Perfect for guiding users through a blank slate or a complex setup process without feeling overly technical.
- **Tech Corporate Communications**: Annual reports, blog headers, and marketing emails that need to feel lively but brand-safe.
- **HR & Recruiting Portals**: Demonstrating a "fun, diverse, and collaborative" remote culture through illustration.
