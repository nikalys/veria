# Handcrafted / Zine

**Definition**: A chaotic, radically informal, and highly textured aesthetic that explicitly mimics physical, analog, DIY (Do-It-Yourself) printing methods. It is universally characterized by mixed-media photo collages, torn paper edges, xerox/photocopy degradation, scribbled Sharpie handwriting, masking tape accents, and a total rejection of pixel-perfect alignment.

**History**: "Zines" (short for fanzines) were self-published, small-circulation magazines born out of the 1970s Punk subculture and the 1990s Riot Grrrl movement. They were literally created by printing out photos, cutting them out with scissors, gluing them to a piece of paper, writing over them with markers, and cheaply photocopying them in black and white. On the web, translating this extreme physical anarchy into HTML/CSS is famously difficult but highly prized by brands aiming for ultimate authenticity and rebellion.

**Variations**:
- **The Collage / Cut-Out Style**: The most prominent feature. Photographs (usually black and white or heavily desaturated) are digitally masked to have jagged, uneven borders as if cut with dull scissors. They are often stacked awkwardly on top of each other.
- **Xerox / Halftone Wash**: The entire site is restricted to harsh black, white, and a single fluorescent accent color (like neon pink). Images are run through aggressive halftone filters, looking like they were printed on a dying 1990s office copier.
- **Scrapbook (Softer Variant)**: Retains the cut-and-paste mentality but softens the punk aggression. Uses textures of brown craft paper, dried flowers, polaroid frames, and watercolor splashes.

**Use Cases**:
- **Skate / Streetwear Brands**: Brands like Vans or Supreme rely heavily on the visual language of the streets and underground subcultures.
- **Indie Music / Record Labels**: Essential for grunge, punk, or alternative electronic acts selling merchandise or announcing tours.
- **Event / Festival Websites**: "Underground" arts festivals or rave promoters rejecting corporate ticketing aesthetics.
