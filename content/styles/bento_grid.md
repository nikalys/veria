# Bento Box / Bento Grid

**Definition**: A distinct visual layout and aesthetic characterized by a strict, asymmetrical grid of isolated, rounded cards (the "compartments" of a bento box). Each card contains a single, focused piece of information (an icon, a stat, a mini-chart, a feature highlight) and is visually separated from the others by equal, distinct gutters. It acts as a highly compartmentalized dashboard view.

**History**: Strongly associated with Apple's product marketing and software design (iOS widgets, Apple Watch faces, iPad home screens) in the late 2010s and early 2020s. It became a massive web design trend around 2022–2023, where entire SaaS landing pages, portfolios, and feature summaries were completely rebuilt utilizing this modular, puzzle-piece structure. It is currently one of the most dominant visual patterns in modern UI.

**Variations**:
- **Feature Bento**: Used primarily on landing pages to summarize a product's capabilities. A massive hero image is replaced by a 3x3 or 4x4 grid of cards, some spanning multiple columns or rows, each highlighting a distinct micro-feature (e.g., a dark mode toggle, a speed graph, an integration logo).
- **Personal Portfolio Bento**: A visual resume where "About Me," "Projects," "Skills," and "Social Links" are all given their own distinctly sized bento boxes on a single, non-scrolling viewport. Popularized by platforms like Bento.me.
- **Utilitarian Dashboard**: The functional application of the style, where distinct widgets (calendar, tasks, weather, analytics) snap into an asymmetrical grid, allowing users to customize their view within strict boundaries.
- **Neobrutalist Bento**: Taking the puzzle-piece layout but applying harsh black borders and solid offset shadows instead of Apple's soft gray dropshadows and subtle blurs.

**Use Cases**:
- **Product Landing Pages**: The most effective way to communicate "we have 10 great features" without forcing the user to scroll through 10 distinct, massive sections.
- **Link-in-Bio / Personal Sites**: Condensing a massive amount of disparate personal information (Spotify playlists, GitHub repos, Twitter feeds) into a single, highly scannable, visually pleasing dashboard.
- **Admin & Analytics Dashboards**: Separating dense, distinct data streams so they don't bleed into one another visually.
- **Mobile First Design**: The bento grid translates flawlessly from desktop to mobile; the boxes simply stack vertically into a single column.
