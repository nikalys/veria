# Cottagecore

**Definition**: An internet aesthetic that romanticizes rural life, pastoral simplicity, and traditional domestic skills. Visually, it relies heavily on soft, earthy, and sun-faded color palettes (sage greens, muted yellows, soft browns), vintage botanical illustrations, delicate serif or handwritten typography, and textures mimicking linen, watercolor, or aged paper.

**History**: Coined on Tumblr in the mid-2010s, it exploded into a massive mainstream cultural movement during the 2020 pandemic lockdowns as a psychological escape from digital burnout and urban isolation. While initially a fashion and lifestyle trend (baking sourdough, wearing linen dresses), it quickly translated into web design for brands attempting to convey slowness, sustainability, and rustic authenticity in a hyper-digital world.

**Variations**:
- **Botanical / Forager**: Heavily reliant on highly detailed, vintage public-domain illustrations of mushrooms, ferns, and insects integrated directly into the UI layout, interacting with the typography.
- **Grandmacore / Vintage Homestead**: Features subtle repeating wallpaper patterns (gingham, faint florals), doily-inspired lace borders, and slightly heavier, nostalgic typography.
- **Fairycore**: The lighter, more magical cousin. Incorporates glowing light leaks, softer pastel greens and pinks, and delicate, almost illegible handwritten script fonts.

**Use Cases**:
- **Artisan / Handcrafted E-Commerce**: The mandatory aesthetic for selling handmade ceramics, organic soaps, knitwear, or slow-fashion clothing.
- **Lifestyle & Recipe Blogs**: Highly prevalent in modern food blogging that emphasizes farm-to-table aesthetics and traditional baking.
- **Sustainable / Eco-Friendly Brands**: Utilizing the visual language of nature to subconsciously communicate "chemical-free" or "ethically sourced."
