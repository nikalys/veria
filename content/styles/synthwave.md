# Synthwave / Retrowave

**Definition**: A dark, neon-soaked, highly stylized aesthetic inspired explicitly by 1980s action movies, video games, and synthesized soundtracks. It is universally defined by deep black or dark purple backgrounds contrasted heavily against glowing neon magenta, cyan, and orange grid lines, often featuring glowing suns melting into the horizon of a low-poly digital landscape.

**History**: Like Vaporwave, Synthwave began as an electronic music microgenre in the mid-2000s (heavily influenced by artists like Kavinsky and the *Drive* movie soundtrack). However, visually, it is entirely distinct from Vaporwave. Where Vaporwave is surreal, pastel, and ironic, Synthwave is dark, aggressive, and highly cinematic. It romanticizes the 1980s vision of the future (fast cars, neon cityscapes, arcade grids) rather than the 1990s vision of consumer computing. It is often incorrectly conflated with Cyberpunk, but Synthwave strictly stops at the 1980s analog synthesizer era.

**Variations**:
- **The Outrun Grid**: The absolute classic implementation. A glowing neon wireframe grid stretching to infinity, converging at a central vanishing point where a massive, horizontally-sliced glowing orange sun sets.
- **Miami Nights (Vice)**: Less focused on glowing wireframes and more focused on neon silhouettes of palm trees, fast cars (like the Lamborghini Countach), and chrome 80s script typography.
- **Dark Synthwave / Darksynth**: A heavier, more aggressive variant bordering on horror. Deeper blacks, blood red neon instead of pink/cyan, and demonic or dystopian 80s movie imagery (e.g., *Terminator* or John Carpenter films).

**Use Cases**:
- **Gaming & Esports UI**: Highly prevalent in indie game UI, Twitch streaming overlays, and tournament graphics.
- **Music Festivals & Promotions**: The default visual language for touring electronic acts or retro-themed nightclub events.
- **Portfolio / "Hacker" Sites**: Developers who want a dark-mode portfolio that specifically evokes highly stylized 80s arcade nostalgia.
