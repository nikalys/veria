# Neumorphism (Soft UI)

**Definition**: A portmanteau of "New Skeuomorphism," Neumorphism relies heavily on playing with light, shadow, and depth to create user interfaces that appear extruded from the background rather than layered on top of it. Elements (like buttons or cards) share the exact same background color as the canvas they rest on, distinguished only by a pair of soft shadows: one light (highlight) and one dark (drop shadow).

**History**: Surged in popularity on Dribbble and UX case studies around late 2019/early 2020 as a visually stunning alternative to the ubiquitous Flat Design and Material Design trends. Unlike traditional skeuomorphism, which tried to copy real-world *textures* (leather, wood), Neumorphism only tried to copy real-world *physics* (how light interacts with matte plastic or clay).

**Variations**:
- **Classic Neumorphism**: Strictly monochromatic (often light gray, warm off-white, or dark mode gray). The background color and element color must match perfectly.
- **Inverted / Pressed Neumorphism**: Instead of extruding *up* from the background (convex), elements are pressed *down* into the canvas (concave) using inner shadows. Usually reserved for active states or form inputs.
- **Glass-Neumorphism**: An experimental fusion combining Neumorphism's solid extruded shapes with Glassmorphism's frosted blur overlays.
- **Micro-Neumorphism**: Using neumorphic principles very sparingly—such as only on specific icon containers or media player controls—while keeping the rest of the layout standard Flat Design.

**Use Cases**:
- **Smart Home / IoT Dashboards**: The " extruded plastic button" aesthetic pairs perfectly with digital controls meant to mimic physical hardware interfaces (like thermostats or remote controls).
- **Audio Players & Synthesizers**: Modernizing classic skeuomorphic audio interfaces by making the knobs and sliders look like sleek molded plastic.
- **Premium Concept Apps**: Often used to showcase high-fidelity design skills because getting the lighting exactly right requires high attention to detail.
- **Fitness / Health Tracking**: The soft, non-threatening aesthetic feels very organic and approachable.

***Accessibility Note***: *Neumorphism is notoriously difficult to make accessible because it relies entirely on very subtle shadow contrast, failing WCAG contrast ratios in most implementations. As a result, it is rarely seen in massive-scale public applications.*
